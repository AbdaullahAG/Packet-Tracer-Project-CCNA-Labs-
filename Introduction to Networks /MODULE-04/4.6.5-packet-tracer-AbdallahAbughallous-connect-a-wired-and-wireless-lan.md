# Packet Tracer: Connect a Wired and Wireless LAN

## 📖 Lab Overview
This activity focuses on selecting the correct cabling for various network devices, including routers, switches, and wireless access points.
You will also explore the difference between logical and physical network topologies within Cisco Packet Tracer.

---

## 🚀 Objectives

### Part 1: Connect to the Cloud
- [ ] Connect **Cloud Eth6** to **Router0 F0/0** (Copper Straight-Through).
- [ ] Connect **Cloud Coax7** to **Cable Modem Port0** (Coaxial).

### Part 2: Connect Router0
- [ ] Connect **Router0 Ser0/0/0** to **Router1 Ser0/0** (Serial).
- [ ] Connect **Router0 F0/1** to **Netacad.pka F0** (Copper Cross-Over).
- [ ] Connect **Router0 Console** to **Config Terminal RS232** (Console).

### Part 3: Connect Remaining Devices
- [ ] Connect **Router1 F1/0** to **Switch F0/1**.
- [ ] Connect **Cable Modem Port1** to **Wireless Router Internet**.
- [ ] Connect **Wireless Router Eth1** to **Family PC F0**.

---

## ✅ Verification & Testing
1. **Connectivity:** Ping `netacad.pka` from the Family PC command prompt.
2. **Web Access:** Open the Web Browser on Family PC and go to `http://netacad.pka`.
3. **Switch Management:** Ping the Switch IP from the Home PC.
4. **Router Status:** Use the Configuration Terminal to run `show ip interface brief` on Router0.

---

## 🔍 Physical Topology Reflection
* **Cloud:** Represents the ISP and external switching.
* **Cabling Colors:** Note that link lights turn **green** for active data connections and **black** for console management connections.
* **Infrastructure:** In the physical view, notice how corporate environments use racks while home networks typically do not.
