---

## Background / Scenario
Every device on an Ethernet LAN is identified by a Layer 2 MAC address. This address is assigned by the manufacturer and stored in the firmware of the NIC. This lab explores the components of a MAC address and how to find this information on a switch and a PC.

---

## Required Resources
* **1 Switch** (Cisco 2960 with Cisco IOS Release 15.2(2) or comparable).
* **1 PC** (Windows with a terminal emulation program, such as Tera Term).

* **Ethernet cables** as shown in the topology.

## Instructions

### Part 1: Configure Devices and Verify Connectivity

#### Step 1: Cable the network.
* Attach the devices and power them on.

#### Step 2: Configure the IPv4 address for the PC.
* Configure the IP and Subnet Mask for **PC-A** via Network Settings.
* From the command prompt, attempt to ping the switch: `ping 192.168.1.2`.

> **Question:** Were the pings successful? Explain.

> *Answer:* (No, because the switch SVI has not been configured or enabled yet).

#### Step 3: Configure basic settings for the switch.
```bash
Switch> enable
