## Part 1: Examine Network Requirements

### Step 1: Determine how many host addresses and subnets are available.
* **How many host addresses are available in a /25 network?**
  126 hosts ($2^{(32-25)} - 2 = 126$).
* **What is the total number of host addresses needed in the topology diagram?**
  80 hosts (40 + 25 + 5 + 4 + 4 + 2).
* **How many subnets are needed in the network topology?**
  6 subnets.

### Step 2: Determine the largest subnet.
* **Subnet description:** BR1 LAN
* **IP addresses required:** 40
* **Subnet mask:** 255.255.255.192 (/26)
* **Total host addresses supported:** 62
* **Can you subnet the 192.168.33.128/25 network?** Yes.
* **Network addresses resulting:** 192.168.33.128/26 and 192.168.33.192/26.
* *Decision: Use 192.168.33.128/26 for BR1 LAN.*

### Step 3: Determine the second largest subnet.
* **Subnet description:** BR2 LAN
* **IP addresses required:** 25
* **Subnet mask:** 255.255.255.224 (/27)
* **Total host addresses supported:** 30
* **Can you subnet the remaining network?** Yes.
* **Network addresses resulting:** 192.168.33.192/27 and 192.168.33.224/27.
* *Decision: Use 192.168.33.192/27 for BR2 LAN.*

### Step 4: Determine the third largest subnet.
* **Subnet description:** BR2 IoT, CCTV, and HVAC LANs.
* **IP addresses required:** 5, 4, 4 respectively.
* **Subnet mask:** 255.255.255.248 (/29)
* **Total host addresses supported:** 6
* **Network addresses resulting:** 192.168.33.224/29, 192.168.33.232/29, 192.168.33.240/29, 192.168.33.248/29.
* *Decisions:*
    * BR2 IoT LAN: 192.168.33.224/29
    * BR2 CCTV LAN: 192.168.33.232/29
    * BR2 HVAC C2 LAN: 192.168.33.240/29

### Step 5: Determine the fourth largest subnet.
* **Subnet description:** BR1-BR2 Link.
* **IP addresses required:** 2
* **Subnet mask:** 255.255.255.252 (/30)
* **Total host addresses supported:** 2
* **Network addresses resulting:** 192.168.33.248/30, 192.168.33.252/30.
* *Decision: Use 192.168.33.248/30 for BR1-BR2 Link.*

#Reflection Question
Can you think of a shortcut for calculating the network addresses of consecutive /30 subnets?
Yes. Since a /30 subnet has a block size of 4, you can simply add 4 to the fourth octet of the current network address to find the next one (e.g., .248, .252, .256...).
